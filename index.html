Common Questions

The current prototype detects airborne chemicals including VOCs and CO₂, skin-surface temperature, and a panel of sweat biomarkers including cortisol and electrolyte markers. Signal classes are configurable—the film's recognition chemistry can be tuned to a target analyte set.

When a signal crosses a pre-set threshold, the film triggers a soft haptic or thermal cue at the skin surface—no sound, no screen, nothing that interrupts you. A full signal log is available on request via a companion interface, but nothing is pushed to you by default.

Smartwatches sample periodically and rely on optical or electrical contact. LEAF's film responds continuously and simultaneously to chemical, thermal, and ambient signals—no sampling interval, no discrete electrodes. It reads the environment and the body in the same breath.

Signal processing happens entirely on-device. No data is transmitted under default settings, and no identifiable information leaves the film without a manual sync you initiate. In pilot deployments, only aggregate anonymised patterns are shared with research partners—never raw individual signal streams.
